Finding a good routine

By Rachel Arthur

Get yourself set up so it’s easy to get work done. If you’re starting out from home on your own, ensure you have a space organised that works for you. And more importantly make sure you have some sort of routine in place so the work flows more easily. Take advantage of that fact you don’t have a commute and get up early – but give yourself relevant breaks. It’s ok to do yoga at 11am. In fact, you should do yoga at 11am.

If you need interaction or once you’ve grown and have a team in place, explore some of your local co-working spaces on offer. I use Huckletree in London – the venues are perfect, the team is incredibly friendly, and the networking is useful too.
Work hard. You are going to have to put your heart and soul into this to make it a reality so be prepared for a lot of slogging ahead. There’s no such thing as an easy ride. Don’t expect a 9-5 deal anymore; if you want your business to succeed you will need to eat, sleep and breathe it for some time. The simple truth is; you get out what you put in. As my Dad always says to me: “You snooze, you lose.”

A motto I often live by is ‘eat the biggest frog first’. If you’re struggling to find motivation one day and that “to do” list is looking more stressful than ever (which happens to all of us), the best thing to do is get the worst task out of the way. Every other frog on the plate will be far more appetising thereafter.
