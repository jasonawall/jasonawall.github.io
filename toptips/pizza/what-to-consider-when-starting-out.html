What to consider when starting out

By Thom and James Elliott, Pizza Pilgrims

We asked ourselves the following questions before we gave up our day jobs and committed ourselves to our new business venture. It’s important to be honest with yourself and go with your gut instinct.

First of all, you need to seriously ask yourself whether you are ready for a job with no off button and no down time. It’s tough going when you start your own business, but persevere and you’ll soon start to see the rewards.

What are you doing that differentiates you from others? It’s difficult to think of an idea that hasn’t already been done, but it’s important to consider what you can give your customers that is unique and improves upon what’s already being offered by others.

Do you know what your strengths and weaknesses are? You will have been asked this question numerous times in past job interviews but when you’re starting out on your own, you become your own boss so be honest with yourself and look to play on your strengths and work on your weaknesses.

Above all, ask yourself: “Why do you want to run your own business?” Don’t just start a business because something’s fashionable right now or because you think you’ll make loads of money. You must be passionate about what you doing in order to have a chance at succeeding.
