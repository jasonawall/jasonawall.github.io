Ask the tough questions

By Luke Edward Hall

Before starting out on your own, you should determine what it is that gives you the edge. Ask yourself: Are there other people doing what I'm doing better than me? Do I have a unique point of view? Can I bring something new to the table?

When setting out on a new venture, whether it’s for business or leisure, you always have to consider whether you will be able to survive financially, as even the greatest ideas can’t get off the ground without funding. Ask yourself whether you will need an additional source of income when you’re first starting out.

Of course, there’s never a right or wrong time to set up a business, but ensure that you’ve thoroughly given thought to whether it’s the right time in your life to start this new journey. Some people will be ready to dive straight in, yet others might feel they need more experience before venturing out on their own. There’s no definite answer, just do what feels right for you.

Starting your own business requires a huge amount of patience, dedication and motivation. From the offset you need to be honest and ask yourself: “Will I have the drive to keep going, even when things don't go right?” Naturally things won't always go right! However, it’s important to not give up, and instead to keep going and learn from your mistakes.
