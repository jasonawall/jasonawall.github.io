Building your brand

by Samsung

You might associate the word ‘brand’ with a logo, design, name or reputation; essentially a marker that separates one business from another. However, too few people recognise the importance of building a positive brand image. Be sure to establish your own unique brand identity from the offset, as this will open you up to more professional opportunities and will help to make business relationships more valuable. Remember: successful entrepreneurs have great branding at the heart of their business.

Tone of Voice

Maintaining consistency across all your business communications is key, as once people know who you are and begin to identify you with a specific area of understanding or expertise, then you’ll be on your way to becoming the go-to person in your industry.

Having a consistent tone will help build your brand but it’s also essential to make sure that it reflects your genuine values and passions. Essentially your brand is a reflection of yourself so when setting the tone, ask yourself what do you believe in? What do you stand for? What are your strengths and weaknesses? Customers want to connect with genuine businesses so make sure your branding is true to yourself.

Building Trust

As a starting point, setting the right tone for your company will put you on the right path to building long term relationships with customers. However, building trust takes time and lot of hard work. Focus on these three things: service, reliability and honesty.

At the end of the day, great service will always matter to your customers, so you can easily build trust here by ensuring you always deliver on your promises. Then, make sure you don’t just deliver on these promises once, but continue to do so time and time again to provide consistency. And finally, be honest: having a truly successful business is based on complete trust. Everyone makes mistakes when they’re starting out so don’t be afraid to admit yours!

Moving with the times

It’s becoming even harder to reach people as the market becomes more and more crowded and your potential customers find themselves bombarded with so many messages from so many different companies. Stand out from the crowd by being innovative, bold and daring when building your brand; don’t try to mimic the look of others, but instead find your own unique voice that allows you to shine through.

But projecting a powerful personal image doesn’t just happen on paper, entrepreneurs also need to keep up with the fast paced world of technology and build their profile through social networks and websites. When drawing up your roadmap for future online brand-building efforts, be sure to consider what kind of content will best serve your mission, how you will remain consistent across the many internet platforms and the ways in which you will engage your audience.

Networking events; some of the most successful entrepreneurs started out by attending at least 10 to 15 events per week. You need to get among people and you need to meet as many as you can! Once you’ve started to build up your contacts, then don’t forget to keep in touch to make sure the relationship stays alive.
