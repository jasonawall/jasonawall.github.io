Finding your story

by Samsung

If there’s a business idea that stays with you that you’re seriously passionate about, then there’s no reason why you can’t turn that dream into a reality. Positive thinking can go a long way to actually getting started, as one of the biggest hurdles people can face is a lack of self-belief – it’s very easy to convince yourself that you can’t do it! The first step is often the hardest but once you’re on that path, you need to start thinking about what your brand stands for and how you communicate that to your customers. And the rewards will be worth it! Samsung research shows that senior decision makers in small businesses believe those who have turned their passions into pay checks have increased job satisfaction (84%), increased creativity (63%) and improved focus at work (59%) as a result of pursuing their passions.

Most entrepreneurs love what they do and as a result, have pushed themselves, put in the hard graft and turned an initial concept into a great product or service for others to enjoy. As you begin to develop your idea into a business venture, think about how your passion runs through your brand story. How does it make your business interesting and different and why should your customers care?

Personal passions

Reflect on your life experiences, characteristics and interests, and consider how these will shine through in the business and form part of your brand story. How does this make your business unique? The more you can weave in your personal passion and story, the more authentic and compelling your business will be and the more impact you will have in your market.

What does your business stand for?

When asked how to define their brand, many start-ups struggle to define who they really are and what their businesses truly stand for. Don’t be afraid to define your purpose for the fear of alienating others – make sure your passion shines through and embed it in your company DNA, as these values serve as the foundation of your brand identity and set you apart from everyone else. 

Think about it now. Would you be able to answer the following questions if put on the spot?

  1. What does your business stand for?
  2. What benefits does your product or service provide?
  3. What do you want to be known for?

Remember: defining your brand will help grow your business through awareness and confidence. Ultimately to help create a loyal customer base, you want them to share your passion and feel a connection to your product or service. The best way to do this is to ensure your business clearly stands for something and that your passion is clearly communicated.

The perfect elevator pitch

Devising a compelling elevator pitch is a challenge every entrepreneur must overcome. Convincing potential shareholders to listen to you is no easy feat so you need to be able to sell your idea in a couple of sentences, remember:

  1. Keep it short and sweet – Can you communicate your pitch in less than sixty seconds?
  2. Easy peasy – Don’t confuse your audience, because once they’re lost, it’s impossible to bring them back!
  3. One-liners – organise your pitch into a few key selling points to hit all the important points. Make every word count.
  4. Question time – the elevator pitch should be a conversation between you and a prospective investor so it’s crucial to invite follow-up questions from them.
  5. Make the ask – Don’t be afraid to invite your potential investors to take action. Ask for a follow-up call or appointment to ensure you keep the conversation moving forward.

Being your own brand ambassador

When your idea is finally beginning to resemble a stable business model and you’ve delivered the perfect elevator pitch to prospective investors, make sure that marketing your brand doesn’t become an afterthought. Get out there and meet your future customers and employees, as the best way for a small business owner to be a brand ambassador is to do what they say they’ll do and deliver an exceptional product or service that meets a need. Only you are responsible for making sure that your brand’s reputation stays intact. Live and breathe your brand, think critically and continue to evaluate and re-evaluate consistently, in order to keep surprising and delighting your customers.
