Social media DOs and DON'Ts

by Samsung

In a world of endless online videos to watch, it doesn’t take long before people get bored, distracted or simply find something else to watch, so as a business you need to be doing everything you can to hold their attention. What’s more, customers are now not only taking to social media to learn more about your brand, but also to see what others are saying about you. We’ve compiled our top dos and don’ts for a positive social media experience to help entrepreneurs succeed.

Be yourself

Social media shouldn’t be a smokescreen for you to hide behind. Start by understanding your identity, your brand’s identity and the voice of who is speaking across all your social media platforms. Relationships with your customers grow best when they’re honest.

Consistency is key

A consistent approach to social media is key to getting started. Start by posting an image at the same time every day and find themes for specific days or events. And remember the hashtag etiquette: keep them short, keep them relevant and keep reusing them! Consistency will also mean people know when to look out for your content, be that a deal on a Monday, a great picture on a Thursday or a re-tweet competition every Friday.

Plan, plan, plan

Planning is key to maintaining an effective social media strategy. Whilst it’s fine to keep an element of spontaneity for those on-the-go moments and to keep things fresh, it’s important to plan the bulk of your posts in advance. This ensures you always have something to say, that you’ve given the bulk of your content some thought and have made it as compelling as possible. In order to reach the most people possible, the best time of day to post is lunchtime from Monday to Thursday, which is when people take a break and check into their social media accounts.

Be part of the #conversation

Stay relevant as a brand by jumping on the news agenda using trending hashtags, as well as posting content linked to weekly hashtags such as #MondayMotivation, #ThrowbackThursday and #FollowFriday. Better still, why not create your own branded hashtag like KitKat’s #HaveaBreak, which has resulted in lots of authentic images of the community enjoying their product? Getting your hashtags right will help you stay current, engage your community and grow your follower base: win, win.

Pictures tell a thousand words

When creating engaging content for your platform, posting images or video is one of the key ways to help drive more engagement and traffic. Remember that your images must appeal directly to your target audience. Share advice, offer quick tips, or tell people your favourite quote, and of course, don’t forget to tag them with a memorable hashtag.
