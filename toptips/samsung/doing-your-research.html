Doing your research

by Samsung

Starting your own business can be like building a house of cards; it requires a lot of patience and dedication, and sometimes you may feel like it’s about to all topple over. Doing your research is key to helping you build a successful business plan and brand, and to enable you to turn a great idea into a great business.

Finding the perfect business idea

You know you want to start a business, but where exactly do you start? For some, the idea has always been there, it’s just about getting started, but many people fall at the first hurdle: finding the perfect business idea. Look at what makes you tick. If you’re going to start up a business, it’s better if there is something about it that you love, so you have the motivation to pursue it. Ask yourself what do you enjoy? What are your passions? What are you really good at? Can you find a way to turn these into a viable business?

Research, research, research…

Somewhere between scribbling your idea on a restaurant napkin and actually turning it into a fully functioning business, market research will form an important part of your preparation and business planning. Being informed and knowing your market will put you in a position of strength, help stop your business idea from crashing at the first hurdle and more importantly, will help you to keep your business goals on track.

There’s a wealth of information and insights out there; as a starting point you can contact industry associations and business schools, read media publications and sites, spend time online absorbing the breadth of information the internet has to offer and attend trade shows and conferences. You want to gain a sense of the type of customer your product or service will serve and what makes them tick. Customers may ultimately like your product, but market research will enable you to determine whether it’s something they really need.

And then insights, insights insights…

Your market research will have undoubtedly uncovered the main competitors to your business, but make time to find out what they’re up to – there’s no harm in becoming a customer to the competition! Ask yourself what are they doing that you could do differently, or better. Moreover, many products or services can fail because of unknown requirements and costs, so spend time talking to experts in your markets and listen out for these hidden challenges.
